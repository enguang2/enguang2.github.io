I am a PhD student in the [Department of Computer Science](https://cs.illinois.edu/) at the **University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign** (UIUC), advised by [Prof. Deepak Vasisht](https://deepakv.web.illinois.edu/). My research centers on **mobile computing**, **indoor localization**, and **wireless sensing systems**. I build systems that enable accurate, infrastructure-free positioning for everyday devices.

Prior to my PhD, I interned on the **Android Location Team at Google** (2025), where I worked on next-generation indoor localization for Google Maps. I hold an M.S. and B.S. (Highest Distinction) in CS/Statistics from UIUC.

<div class="section-title">Research</div>

My work focuses on making accurate localization available everywhere — even indoors where GPS fails — without requiring specialized infrastructure. Key themes:

- **Indoor Localization**: Scalable, infrastructure-free positioning using standard Wi-Fi (RTT/FTM), IMU sensors, and probabilistic/optimization-based backends.
- **Sensor Fusion**: Combining inertial, RF, and visual signals (PDR, Kalman filtering, EKF) for robust state estimation on mobile devices.
- **Wireless Systems**: Characterizing and exploiting non-cooperative Wi-Fi behavior, multipath modeling, and clock-offset-robust ranging.
- **Robotics & Security**: UAV swarm coordination, GPS spoofing detection, and SLAM.
